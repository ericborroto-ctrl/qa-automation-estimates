Best Practices/Estimating Guidelines
State Farm Insurance Claims - GC Claims
When responding to State Farm Assignments, Network Contractors must adhere to the
following minimum SLAs, best practices and estimate writing guidelines promulgated by
State Farm.
Service Level Agreements
Standard Claims
o Contact
• Within two (2) hours from when assignment is received
• Update AlacNet and XactAnalysis with contact date and time
o On-Site and Estimate Upload
• The contractor will conduct the on-site inspection and submit the repair
estimate for review within 48 hours from the time contact was made
with the insured
▪ Once Inspection is scheduled, please update AlacNet and
XactAnalysis with the inspection date and time
Once the inspection is complete, please add a detailed scope comment of your findings.
Business Hours
o Normal business hours are defined as 7 A.M. to 7 P.M. within the local time
zone of the State Farm policyholder loss location.

The items below are meant as a guide when estimating. If there are items that need
additional documentation to describe what is needed, please place a comment within

Repairs/Structure Rebuild
General
o The General Contractor will call the State Farm Claim Handler if there are any
unusual circumstances
• Source of loss is different than what was reported and provided in the
loss referral
• Pre-existing damage
• Evidence of long-term mold or rot
• Circumstances that may warrant further investigation by the Claim Rep
to verify coverage is in force, such as heat not maintained in the
structure, evidence that damage was not sudden and accidental, etc.
• It is the responsibility of the Claim Rep to make coverage decisions.
• If hazardous material is discovered- mold/lead/asbestos.
• If cabinets need to be demoed/removed
• Any customer complaints
• All matching issues must be discussed with the claim handler (i.e.
estimating more than the damaged area)
o Contractor must consult with the claim handler to resolve any
issues surrounding matching and un-matching material or with
products where there is no availability or where availability is
limited
o Opening/Closing Statements
• Do not include any opening/closing statements on your Xactimate
estimate
o Adjuster Codes
• Do not include the adjuster’s code on your Xactimate estimate
• This will be listed under the Claim Rep’s information in the Xactimate
assignment you receive
▪ Change the Claim Rep’s information to the Estimator’s name

o Depreciation
• Contractors should not be adding depreciation to the repair estimate.
The Claim Reps will add this into the estimates if applicable
o Deductibles
• Contractors should not add the deductible on the Xactimate estimate.
The Claim Rep will add this into the estimate if the deductible is being
applied to the estimate
o Company Headers
• Estimate should include your company name, address & phone
number
• Do not include the company’s Tax ID
o Writing your estimate- the preferred order when estimating
• Interior
▪ Write the estimate from the floors, walls, ceilings
• Exterior
▪ Write the estimate from the ground, siding, roof
o Labor Minimums
• Labor minimum charges should be applied only for trades that do not
overlap and a separate tradesperson is needed to perform the services
needed
• Should be applied at the end of the estimate vs using the labor
minimum line item charges in the estimate
• Do not apply labor minimum/adjusted amount for items being
completed b other tradesperson(s) who will likely be on the job site and
not need a specialist
• If multiple Labor Minimums are applied, contractor must add a note
into AlacNet to support the need for the multiple labor minimums
o Labor Only (LAB)
• Hourly Charges should only be used in extenuating circumstances
• Should only be estimated if a specific line item entry for that operation
does not exist
• A line item note (F9) is required to explain any LAB items
o LAB SUPERR/LAB SUPERC
• Generally, LAB SUPERR is not needed or applicable for most
residential jobs and I not automatic for commercial jobs. Thorough
explanation required if estimated

o Overhead and Profit
• O&P is based on the repair complexity, the participation of the
additional trades, the coordination and scheduling efforts associated
with the various trade subcontractors
• Exceptions for O&P must be explained and documented within the
claim notes
• No cumulative O&P
• O&P is not applicable on non-complex repairs/losses
• No O&P on the following services:
▪ Emergency Services (tarp, temp roof repairs, mitigation)
▪ Content Cleaning and Restoration
▪ Licensing
▪ Permit Fees
• The General Contractor must have significant involvement in the daily
operation of the repairs
• Claim Handler may request additional documentation of the O&P from
the assigned contractor
o Hourly Charges
• Hourly Charges should only be used in extenuating circumstances
▪ Use only if specific line item entry for that task does not exist in
the price list
▪ Contractor must enter a F9 note under that line item, explaining
the need of the hourly charge.
o High/Premium Quality Items
• High/Premium grade materials must be documented with a line item
note (F9) and photos.
o Scope Comment- Provide as much detail as possible:
• What was the cause of loss?
• What areas are affected?
• What work will be performed?
• Was there flooring affected, if so, why type?
• Are there any coverage concerns?
• Is there possible subrogation?
o Travel Charges
• Estimate should not include any line items relating to contractor travel

Hazardous Materials
o Mold Remediation, asbestos abatement and/or Lead Abatement are excluded
from the Repair program
• If the contractor discovers asbestos and/or mold at the loss, the
contractor should contact the assigned State Farm Claim Rep
immediately to discuss.
▪ NOTE: Testing for mold, asbestos and/or lead should be billed
directly to State Farm for payment.
o Mitigation, Contents Cleaning or Unassigned Services
• The contractor will not perform or provide any services outside the
Repairs services. This includes water/fire mitigation, contents cleaning
or restoration of personal property, mold remediation, lead
remediation, and/or asbestos abatement, unless instructed by the
State Farm Claim Rep.
• If the insured requests the contractor to perform any of the services
above, refer the insured back to their Claim Rep to discuss
